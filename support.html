BaseLayer – Support

Welcome to the support page for BaseLayer, the adaptive aerobic base builder for runners. BaseLayer connects to a Bluetooth heart-rate monitor, tracks your run data, and helps guide your personal aerobic base training in real time.

At the core of BaseLayer is a fitness signal based on grade-adjusted pace divided by heart rate. The app uses this, together with proprietary physiological modelling, to guide your aerobic base training and help determine when you have done enough productive aerobic work for the day.

BaseLayer is designed to guide aerobic base training. It does not replace weekly long runs or speed sessions. Instead, it helps guide the aerobic foundation that supports progress across the rest of your training.

FAQ – Quick Answers

  • My heart-rate monitor will not connect.
    Make sure your monitor is awake, worn correctly, and close to your phone. Try opening the monitor connection screen again and rescanning. If your monitor is already connected elsewhere, disconnect it from the other app or device first.
  • Why does the app request Location permission?
    BaseLayer uses location and movement data during runs to measure speed and related run signals. On some devices and operating systems, location permission may also be required for Bluetooth scanning or connection features to work correctly.
  • Why does the app need Bluetooth permission?
    Bluetooth permission is required so the app can scan for, connect to, and reconnect to your compatible heart-rate monitor.
  • Why is vibration or haptic feedback not working?
    Check that your device supports vibration, that haptics are enabled in system settings, and that your phone is not limiting vibration behaviour.
  • My subscription did not unlock premium features.
    Tap Restore purchases on the subscription screen if you already have a valid App Store subscription. If that does not work, contact support and include the device model and a short description of the issue.
  • How does the first run work?
    Your first run is used to calibrate the app to your physiology. It is important that this first run matches the distance of your typical daily run and is done at your usual training pace. After that, BaseLayer uses that calibration to guide your personal aerobic base target in future runs.
  • How does BaseLayer measure fitness?
    BaseLayer measures fitness progress using grade-adjusted pace divided by heart rate. If you can run faster at the same heart rate, or hold the same grade-adjusted pace at a lower heart rate, your aerobic fitness signal has improved.
  • Does BaseLayer replace speedwork or long runs?
    No. BaseLayer is designed to guide aerobic base training. It does not replace weekly long runs or speed sessions. Instead, it helps guide the aerobic foundation that supports progress across the rest of your training.
  • Will the app remember my monitor?
    Yes. Once connected, BaseLayer is designed to remember your preferred heart-rate monitor and reconnect to it automatically when possible.
